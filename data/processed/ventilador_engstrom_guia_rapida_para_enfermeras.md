Standby

Monitoring and ventilation cease when the ventilator is placed into Standby. Follow the method for “Starting ventilation” to exit Standby.

WARNING  The patient will not be ventilated when in Standby.

To enter Standby:

1. Disconnect the patient from the circuit.

2. Push the  Standby  button located in the upper left hand section of the display (Figure 1).

3. Select  Standby  (see the picture on the following page)

Note:  If you select  Standby  without disconnecting the patient or if you occlude the circuit, the alarm “Patient connected” will appear on the screen.

Park circuit  (only from software version 5.x)

Use this function to allow the patient circuit to be occluded without the ventilator alarming while in standby. This function allows the patient circuit to be hygienically protected while waiting to connect the patient. Removing the circuit occlusion will clear the Park Circuit status.

The message “Circuit Parked” appears on the screen while in this mode.

WARNING  The patient will not be ventilated when the circuit is parked.

To enter Park Circuit:

1. Following the procedure to entering “Standby”.

2. Occlude the patient circuit.

3. Select  Park Circuit  (see the picture above): the ventilator stops alarming.

As soon as the circuit is being opened, the ventilator turns to Standby position automatically and the alarms can be activated again.

Suction routine

The Suction Procedure provides:

• Pre-oxygenation with 100% O 2  to the patient for 2 minutes

• Automatically switching to Standby for 2 minutes

• Post-oxygenation with 100% O 2  for 2 minutes.

Note:  The suction procedure is not to be used with in-line suction devices. The suction procedure requires the patient to be disconnected in order for the procedure to operate correctly.

Suction Procedure:

1. The Procedure button is located on the left side of the display (Figure 1). Push the  Procedures  button.

2. Turn the ComWheel to select  Suction  (Figure 7).

Figure 7

3. The system will immediately start delivering 100% O 2  for 2 minutes or until the patient is disconnected from the ventilator. A message appears on the right top portion of the screen describing which step of the suction procedure is active (Figure 8).

Aeroneb PRO nebulizer maintenance

Sterilize the nebulizer prior to the ﬁ rst use on a patient. Clean and sterilize the nebulizer between patients.

1. Remove the nebulizer and the adapters from the ventilator circuit.

2. Disassemble the nebulizer unit and adapters into individual components (Figure 16).

Figure 16

CAUTION:  Do not autoclave or submerge the nebulizer cable.

3. Remove the ﬁ ller cap from the nebulizer and empty any excess liquid.

4. Clean all of the parts in warm water and a mild liquid detergent.

5. Shake the excess water from the parts, and allow the parts to air dry.

CAUTION:  Do not reassemble parts prior to autoclaving.

6. Sterilize the components.

• To steam sterilize, autoclave the wrapped parts using steam sterilization pre-vacuum cycle at 132°C to 135°C for 3 to 4minutes with a drying cycle.

• To sterilize with hydrogen peroxide gas plasma, place wrapped parts in a STERRAD 100S System and use the long cycle.
