 Ambient air is drawn in via the respiratory gas filter from the blower unit and compressed.  The speed of the radial fans in the blower unit is regulated by the controller.  To cool the device and the respiratory gas, the ambient air is drawn into the housing by a fan.  Sensors continuously measure the ambient temperature and humidity.  The respiratory gas is cooled by way of a heat exchanger.  The waste heat from the device and respiratory gas cooling is routed out of the housing by a fan.  The respiratory gas is fed to the Evita Infinity V500 or Babylog VN500 ventilation unit.

GS500-DC

 GS500-DC is driven by an 24V-DC/DC-converter instead of an 220V power supply.

 Is the workplace running on main supply, will the GS500-DC get the power from power supply M7.3. When the main supply fails, the GS-500-DC get the power from the batteries of the workplace:

 Workplace with GS500-DC without PS500: the internal NiMH-battery of V-unit delivers 24V to GS500-DC. (Total running time of workstation is reduced from 30min to max. 15min!)

 Workplace with GS500-DC and PS500: the V-Unit doesn't have the internal battery. The batteries from PS500 are connected to M7.3. The GS500-DC is connected via a cable between M7.3 and PCB Power and will get the power via M7.3 from the batteries in the PS500. Running time: approx. 120min

GS500 – DC Installation

 Dräger has provided a conversion kit, with it an GS500-AC (230V) must be converted to GS500-DC (24V-DC/DC-converter).

 This conversion from GS500-AC (230V) to GS500-DC (24V) has to be done according TSB 08 (Class 2) until 31.12.2013!

 A workplace with the option „GS500-DC“ has a new PCB on M16 installed. This PCB (PBA Remote) is switching of the GS500-DC, when the workplace is shutting down. (The GS500-DC has no power switch.)
